Turning the Trolley

- Article talks about the trolley problem, specifically answers to the problem by Alexander Friedman who is an MIT grad(?)
- First talks about the origin of the problem from Phillipa Foot and then Thomson's take on it
	- Phillipa made Driver Two Choices and Judge Two Choices
	- Thomson made Bystander Two Choices and Fat Man (footbridge problem)
- Phillipa posits two principles
	- Letting 5 die vs killing one
		- You must let 5 die if it requires killing one
	- Killing 5 vs killing one
		- You must let one die if the alternative is killing 5
- Negative vs positive duties
	- non-interference vs aid, where positive is considered less weighty than negative
- ceteris paribus - with other things being equal
- a fortiori - implies an argument from a stronger argument
- Phillipa Foot's problem - Why can we not choose option 2 in Fat Man, but we can in Driver?
